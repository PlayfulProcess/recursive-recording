# Alice's Adventures Under Ground (1864)

The manuscript that became *Wonderland*. Charles Lutwidge Dodgson (Lewis Carroll) first
told the story to the three Liddell sisters — Lorina, Alice, and Edith — during a rowing
trip up the Thames from Folly Bridge to Godstow on **4 July 1862**, accompanied by his
friend Robinson Duckworth [@wp_carroll]. Alice Liddell asked him to write it down.

Carroll produced a fair-copy manuscript, *Alice's Adventures Under Ground*, in his own hand
and with **37 of his own pen drawings**, completing it on 13 September 1864 and giving it to
Alice Liddell as a gift later that year [@wikisource_underground][@morgan_underground]. The
title page of the published facsimile reads "WITH THIRTY-SEVEN ILLUSTRATIONS BY THE AUTHOR"
[@gutenberg_19002].

The original manuscript is held by the **British Library** as Add MS 46700
[@morgan_underground]. A facsimile is freely available as Project Gutenberg eBook **#19002**
[@gutenberg_19002] — the correct anchor for this item (note: PG #28885 is the *Rackham 1907*
edition, not this manuscript).

**Confidence.** High on authorship, the 37 drawings, the 1864 date, and BL provenance. The
often-repeated month-level specifics ("begun November 1862," "given November 1864") are
*medium* confidence — the year and the gift framing are solid, the exact months less so
[@morgan_underground].
