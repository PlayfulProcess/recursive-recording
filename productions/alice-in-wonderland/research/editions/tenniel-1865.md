# Alice's Adventures in Wonderland — first edition (1865/66)

The canonical *Alice*. Carroll expanded *Under Ground* into *Wonderland* and commissioned
**Sir John Tenniel**, the principal *Punch* cartoonist, to illustrate it. Tenniel produced
**42 illustrations**, cut as wood-engravings by the **Dalziel Brothers**; Carroll directed
the collaboration closely and famously paid for and controlled production himself under a
commission model with **Macmillan & Co.** [@aiw_tenniel][@britannica_tenniel][@lcs_publishing].

The first printing of **2,000 copies** (Oxford University Press, mid-1865) was **suppressed**:
Tenniel was dissatisfied with the quality of the printed illustrations, and Carroll recalled
it [@hermitage_firstedition][@ransom_tenniel]. The genuine first *published* edition was
reprinted (Richard Clay) and issued in late 1865 with a title page **dated 1866**
[@hermitage_firstedition]. The rejected sheets were sold to **D. Appleton & Co.** of New York
and reissued there with a new title page [@umd_appleton]. Only about two dozen copies of the
suppressed 1865 issue survive [@hermitage_firstedition].

Tenniel's designs became the "canonical" Alice that every later illustrator answered to. For a
public-domain text-and-image anchor, use **Wikisource's 1866 Macmillan edition**
[@wikisource_wonderland].

**Confidence.** High throughout. The "2,000 copies" suppressed figure is well attested; avoid
conflating it with later print runs.
