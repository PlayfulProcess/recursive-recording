# The Nursery "Alice" (1890)

A version for "Children aged from Nought to Five," with **twenty** of Tenniel's *Wonderland*
illustrations **enlarged, redrawn, and coloured**. The colouring of the interior plates was
done/supervised by **Tenniel himself** — surviving proofs carry his handwritten notes calling
for changes after the initial proofs came back too dark — and the book was engraved and
**colour-printed by Edmund Evans**, the leading Victorian colour wood-engraver
[@wp_nursery][@morgan_nursery][@vam_nursery].

**E. Gertrude Thomson's role was the cover only.** The V&A's transcription of the title page
reads: "…twenty coloured enlargements from Tenniel's illustrations… *The cover designed and
coloured by E. Gertrude Thomson*" [@vam_nursery]. The widely-copied claim that Thomson coloured
the interior plates (it appears on her own Wikipedia article) is contradicted by the V&A, the
Morgan, the main *Nursery "Alice"* article, and the standard reference Cohen & Wakeling,
*Lewis Carroll and his Illustrators* — treat it as **a known error** [@wp_nursery][@wp_thomson].

Carroll rejected the first ~10,000 sets of sheets that Evans printed as "far too bright and
gaudy" and had them reprinted on whiter paper with toned-down colour
[@rareantique_nursery]. Production was completed in **1889** (a few presentation copies were
made up that year; the Morgan dates the colour proofs "[1889]"), with the trade issue dated
**1890** [@wp_nursery][@morgan_nursery].

**Confidence.** High. The corrected attribution (Tenniel + Evans for the plates; Thomson for
the cover) is supported by a museum title-page transcription and an archive's primary proofs.
