# Alice — illustrated by Arthur Rackham (1907)

The most celebrated of the post-copyright editions. When the Tenniel-era copyright lapsed,
**William Heinemann** commissioned **Arthur Rackham**, the leading golden-age illustrator, to
re-imagine *Alice*. Published in **1907** with **13 colour plates** (with captioned tissue
guards) plus black-and-white line drawings — the first Rackham book to distribute its plates
through the text [@nocloo_rackham][@sancho_rackham].

It is the one early reillustration consistently regarded as an enduring rival to Tenniel. The
public-domain facsimile is **Project Gutenberg #28885** [@gutenberg_28885] — the correct anchor
for this edition (not Tenniel).

**Confidence.** High on illustrator, publisher, year, and plate count.
