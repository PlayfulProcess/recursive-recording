# Lewis Carroll (Charles Lutwidge Dodgson), 1832–1898

Oxford mathematician at Christ Church (matriculated 1851; mathematical lectureship from 1856),
and amateur photographer of note [@mactutor_carroll][@wp_carroll][@bodleian_carroll_photo]. He
told the *Alice* story to the Liddell sisters on a boat trip on **4 July 1862**, then wrote and
illustrated *Alice's Adventures Under Ground* (1864) for Alice Liddell before expanding it into
*Wonderland* (1865), whose illustrator Tenniel he directed closely
[@wp_carroll][@britannica_carroll]. Died 14 January 1898; the lapse of his text's copyright in
1907 triggered the wave of new illustrated editions.
